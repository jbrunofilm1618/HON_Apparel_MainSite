HŌN comes from the Greek text ὁ ὤν (ho ōn), which appears in the Septuagint translation of Exodus 3:14 — God's self-revelation to Moses at the burning bush: "I AM WHO I AM." It's the oldest surviving declaration of divine identity in the Judeo-Christian tradition, predating denominational divisions.
